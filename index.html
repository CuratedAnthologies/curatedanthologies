Before you write

What kinds of stories are we looking for?

We're looking for stories about real people. Not celebrities. Not public figures. Just people who mattered. (Tap options below to see dynamic excerpts)

A grandparent
"She never went to high school, but she could estimate the cargo weight of a shipping vessel just by seeing how it sat in the harbor..."
A parent
"He spent thirty years working the overnight postal routes, packing an extra lunch every single Tuesday just in case his loader was hungry..."
A teacher
"When everyone else suggested I look into the trades, she sat me down after the bell and gave me a college application she paid for out of her pocket..."
A sibling
"She held onto secrets like iron safes. Growing up in that small apartment, she built a whole imaginary world so I didn't see the struggles..."
A friend
"We didn't talk every day, but during the coldest winter of my life, he showed up on my front porch with a toolkit and an ancient space heater..."
Someone you admire
"She was the town librarian who managed to remember every single child's reading preference for nearly half a century..."
A neighbor
"Mr. G would intentionally clear his driveway slowly just so he could keep an eye out for when my car battery needed a cold-start boost..."
Someone you've lost
"The world carries on completely unbothered, unaware that its gentlest accent and finest laugh vanished last September..."

The story doesn't need to be dramatic.
Sometimes the smallest acts change us the most.
